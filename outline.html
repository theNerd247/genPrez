What is A Version Control System?

A set of tools aimed at

  • knowing who is doing what
  • fixing mistakes
  • collaborating in parallel

Git is A VCS

How does Git work?

Keeping Track of History

  • In order for Git to solve the 3 problems we’ve discussed it needs a way to keep track of the history of your projects

  • It does this by seeing your project history as a timeline of photos

Git uses a sequence of “photos” or snapshots to track the history of your files

What’s a snapshot?

What your files “look like” at a given point in time

Making a Snapshot

Act

  1. Have the students draw 2 stick figures and write a single letter name underneath them. Modify
  2. Then have them draw a simple stage under the stick figures Stage
  3. Make hand motion as if you’re taking a photo and saving it to the time line Snapshot or commit
  4. Have the students write a 3 word description and a time above the photo - Explain this is so we can remember when the photo was made and what was going on
  1. Modify your files
  2. Stage your modified files
  3. Take a snapshot!

Review

Ask

  1. What are the 3 steps for making a snapshot?
  2. What kind of “things” can you put in a snapshot? (What kind of files?)

Undoing your Mistakes

Act

  1. Have the students imagine there’s something wrong with their most recent photo.
  2. Have them make another snapshot that branches off of the 2nd photo.
  3. Have them make a new photo that merges the most recent photo and the last photo made.
  • Checkout the photo that you want to “restart from”
  • Make a new snapshot
  • Merge the two tips of the timeline together by making a new “merge” snapshot
